What's the CINC?

"Power is considered by many to be a central concept in explaining conflict, and six indicators - military expenditure, military personnel, energy consumption, iron and steel production, urban population, and total population - are included in this data set. It serves as the basis for the most widely used indicator of national capability, CINC (Composite Indicator of National Capability) and covers the period 1816-2012." -- UC Davis Correlates of War Project

So... what's this project?

The above is the CINC graphed for five countries, China, Japan, U.S., UK, and Russia, from 1860-2007. We've framed a lot of our understanding of Modern Chinese History in relation to the actions of China's neighbors -- e..g how China reacted to Japan's sudden rise in power during the Meiji restoration and its influence on China's literary reformation, the role of treaty ports and spheres of influence in Chinese rebellions. I wanted to use this dataset of 'power' to ask questions about events that we as historians use power dynamics to explain and understand.
